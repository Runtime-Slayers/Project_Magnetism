TITLE
============================================================
Design and Analysis of Axial Flux Dual-Rotor Permanent Magnet
Generators with YASA Topology: Electromagnetic Modeling and
Performance Evaluation

============================================================
ABSTRACT
============================================================
This paper presents the electromagnetic design and performance
analysis of an axial flux dual-rotor permanent magnet generator
with Yokeless and Segmented Armature (YASA) topology. A
mathematical model based on Faraday's law, Lorentz force
equations, and magnetic circuit theory is developed to
characterize the generator's electromagnetic behavior. The key
design parameters include an 8-pole, 12-slot configuration with
NdFeB N42 permanent magnets (Br = 1.32 T), a 1 mm air gap, and
concentrated three-phase windings. Analytical computations predict
an air-gap flux density of 1.00 T, a phase RMS voltage of 181.3 V,
and a phase current of 10.60 A at 1500 RPM, yielding an output
power of 5419 W with an estimated efficiency of 94.2%. A
comparative analysis against an equivalent radial flux topology
demonstrates that the YASA configuration achieves a 5.6x
improvement in power density (1.259 kW/kg versus 0.225 kW/kg) and
a 47.9% reduction in material cost ($212 versus $407). Cogging
torque analysis via Fourier decomposition quantifies the LCM-based
harmonic suppression (LCM(8,12) = 24) and the effect of 15-degree
magnet skew on torque ripple reduction. Practical design guidelines
for small-scale wind generation and vehicular integrated
starter-generator applications are discussed.
